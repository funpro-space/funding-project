# Founder Narrative Validation Guidelines

This document outlines the evaluation criteria and standards for validating artisan, farm, craft, and regional production projects on the platform. The AI evaluation engine refer to these rules to analyze founder stories, determine if their businesses qualify, identify missing details, and evaluate industry expertise.

---

## 1. Operational Strategy & Core Creation
This pillar evaluates the foundation of how items or crops are produced.
*   **Artisanal Integrity:** Focus on small-batch production, traditional/handcrafted techniques, and rejection of industrial/mass-produced shortcuts.
*   **Supply Chain Transparency:** Sourcing of raw ingredients or materials should be traceable, short, and ethical.
*   **Tools & Equipment:** Explains the specific machinery, workspace layout, and tools used in daily operations.
*   **Viability:** Demonstrates a realistic plan to sell or distribute goods, showing economic sustainability without relying solely on grants.

---

## 2. Process Sustainability & Environmental Integrity
This pillar evaluates the environmental footprint and circular economy practices of the business.
*   **Resource Management:** Active conservation of water, waste reduction, and (for farms) active preservation of soil health.
*   **Upcycling & Circularity:** Creative channels for recycling waste or upcycling scraps (e.g., composting agricultural residue, utilizing timber offcuts for smaller products).
*   **Low-Impact Materials:** Use of non-toxic, organic, biodegradable, or salvaged/regenerative raw inputs.
*   **Ecosystem Harmony:** Practices that respect local biodiversity, such as avoiding harmful pesticides, polyculture farming, or minimal mechanical site disturbance.

---

## 3. Real-World Community Betterment
This pillar evaluates how deeply integrated the business is within the local social fabric and regional economy.
*   **Local Jobs & Fair Labor:** Creation of regional employment opportunities, fair-trade compensation, and safe labor conditions.
*   **Short Supply Chains:** Percentage of raw inputs sourced within a local radius (typically <100 miles) to circulate wealth in the local economy.
*   **Cultural Preservation:** Active preservation of historical, indigenous, or traditional production methods that might otherwise face obsolescence.
*   **Knowledge Sharing & Community Hubs:** Providing educational tours, hosting public workshops, running apprenticeships, or offering communal resource sharing.

---

## 4. Craft Mastery, Experience & Expertise
This pillar evaluates the credibility, background, and capability of the founder(s).
*   **Years of Practice:** Expertise in the specific craft, farming technique, or creative trade.
*   **Educational / Apprenticeship Heritage:** Formal training, apprenticeships, or self-directed study under established mentors.
*   **Mentorship & Passing the Torch:** Active involvement in training apprentices, hiring novices, or teaching workshops to pass on heritage skills.
*   **Validation of Expertise:** Evidence of reputation, certifications, past projects, or community recognition of their mastery.

---

## 5. Summary & Interpretation Framework
The AI must construct a holistic, clear interpretation and summary of the user's business. It should:
1.  **Identify Core Operations:** What they do and create in a well-organized manner.
2.  **Estimate Expertise:** Inferred years of experience and level of mastery (Apprentice, Professional, Master, or Unknown) based on their text.
3.  **Validate Qualification:** Assess if the business meets the standards under each pillar.
4.  **Pinpoint Gaps:** Identify what is missing or ambiguous, and suggest supplemental questions.
5.  **Bespoke Identity & Personalization:**
    *   **Tagline:** Generate a highly polished, catchy, and inspirational 1-sentence tagline of under 10 words (e.g., "Handweaving Heritage Linens for Modern Homes"). This tagline must be personalized and distinct from the raw summary, avoiding generic phrases.
    *   **Card Suggestions:** Provide highly personalized 1-sentence positive remarks or specific highlights for each of the other cards (`experienceProfile`, `validationMetrics`, `ecoGuidelines`, and `registrationStatus`) instead of generic, repetitive, or boilerplate network-wide messages. This ensures that every card displays a highly relevant, custom insight tailored precisely to their business context.
    *   **Strict Diversity & Non-Repetition Mandate:** 
        *   **No Boilerplate:** Never generate generic placeholder statements like "Total synergy achieved. Your workspace parameters are fully optimized for the network."
        *   **No Verbatim Mirroring:** Never copy and paste the same paragraph or long narrative block (e.g. description of salvaged timber, carpentry apprenticeships, or sustainability details) across multiple card suggestions, taglines, or tags.
        *   **Unique Focus per Card:** Each of the 4 card suggestions must be a completely unique, freshly written 1-sentence insight focusing *strictly* on the theme of that card. Do not repeat the same words or phrases across cards.
        *   **No Duplicated Slogans:** The tagline must not appear anywhere inside the card suggestions or overall summary. Keep them entirely distinct.
