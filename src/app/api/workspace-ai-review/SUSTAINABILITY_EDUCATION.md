# Sustainability Education & Evaluation Standards

This guide outlines the metrics, evaluation rules, and scoring logic for assessing the environmental integrity and sustainability of artisan and regional production projects. The AI engine uses these definitions to calculate a project's **Sustainability Index (1-5)** and its sub-metrics.

---

## 1. Overall Sustainability Index (Scale 1-5)

The Sustainability Index represents a comprehensive evaluation of a project's ecological footprint, circular practices, and commitment to the environment.

*   **Score 1: Initial / High Impact**
    *   *Characteristics:* Heavy reliance on conventional resources, high waste creation, untraceable or long-distance raw material sourcing, and no specific eco-friendly practices mentioned.
    *   *Education:* The project lacks foundational sustainability awareness. Urgent attention is needed to audit waste streams and transition to local/organic suppliers.
*   **Score 2: Developing / Aware**
    *   *Characteristics:* Small awareness of footprint, simple recycling/composting, but lacks systemic practices. Raw inputs are conventional, and chemical usage is unmonitored.
    *   *Education:* Good first steps taken. Focus should shift to systematically tracking water/energy use and substituting toxic inputs with organic/biodegradable alternatives.
*   **Score 3: Established / Eco-Standard**
    *   *Characteristics:* Active conservation of resources, conscious waste reduction, upcycling of major scraps, and largely local or non-toxic material sourcing.
    *   *Education:* Solid ecological standing. To reach higher tiers, the business should explore closed-loop systems (zero-waste) and regenerative techniques (like polyculture or organic soil building).
*   **Score 4: Advanced / Circular**
    *   *Characteristics:* High degree of circularity (e.g., upcycles >80% of waste), use of renewable energy, local short supply chains (<100 miles), and active biodiversity support.
    *   *Education:* Exemplary circular model. The focus is now on mentoring other regional businesses, formalizing carbon/water audits, and implementing restorative/regenerative farming.
*   **Score 5: Master / Regenerative**
    *   *Characteristics:* Fully closed-loop zero-waste operations, 100% renewable energy, net-positive environmental impact, regenerative organic soils, or certified biodegradable/salvaged outputs.
    *   *Education:* Pinnacle of planetary stewardship. These projects actively restore local ecosystems (healing soil, cleaning water, fostering wild habitats) and act as regional hubs of ecological heritage.

## 2. Key Sustainability Sub-Metrics

To provide transparent feedback, we evaluate three core dimensions of environmental action:

### A. Resource Efficiency (Scale 1-5)
Measures the consumption rate and source of water, energy, and raw materials.
*   **Target:** Transitioning to rainwater harvesting, solar/wind power, and low-embodied-energy raw inputs.
*   *Score 1:* High-impact inputs, municipal-only water with no conservation, conventional grid electricity.
*   *Score 3:* Active energy conservation, localized raw inputs, water-efficient tools or processes.
*   *Score 5:* 100% renewable energy (solar/wind/biomass), rainwater/greywater closed loops, local/biodegradable inputs.

### B. Circularity & Upcycling (Scale 1-5)
Measures how waste and production scrap are kept within the value loop instead of entering landfills.
*   **Target:** Turning leftover scraps (timber offcuts, textile clippings, crop waste) into new high-value products or soil nutrients.
*   *Score 1:* Linear "take-make-waste" model. All waste goes to conventional trash bins.
*   *Score 3:* Composting of organic scraps, periodic upcycling or selling of production leftovers.
*   *Score 5:* Systemic zero-waste. 100% of production byproducts are upcycled, composted, or returned to the earth beneficially.

### C. Ecosystem Harmony (Scale 1-5)
Measures the project's impact on soil health, local biodiversity, and surrounding wild habitats.
*   **Target:** Eliminating synthetic pesticides/fertilizers, cultivating native species, polyculture farming, and minimal site disturbance.
*   *Score 1:* Conventional chemical fertilizer/dye usage, monoculture planting, heavy mechanical disturbance.
*   *Score 3:* Organic-first approach, minimal synthetic inputs, active efforts to preserve nearby native trees/plants.
*   *Score 5:* Regenerative polyculture or organic certification, active restoration of local wildlife corridors, natural plant-based non-toxic dyes, zero synthetic chemicals.
